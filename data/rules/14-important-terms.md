## Health Points

When a monster is reduced to **0 Health Points**, they are removed from play.

## Emotional Instability Tests

Some abilities might require a monster to roll an [[kw:emotional-instability|Emotional Instability]] Test. To do so, find the Emotional Instability stat (EmI) on their Monster Card, **then roll 2d6**: if the number is equal to or greater than their EmI, the Emotional Instability test is passed! If not, the test is failed, and **your monster is having a rough time.**

## Energy

Both players start the game with **10 [[kw:energy|Energy]]**. Energy is shared between all monsters on your team, and can be used on various Special Abilities (these are listed on Monster Cards). **A player can never have more than 10 Energy.**

## Special Abilities

Special Abilities are used at a variety of different times. Most of them are used when it's your turn, but many can be used during your opponent's turn too. **The Energy cost of Special Abilities generally ranges from 0 to 3.**

*They greatly vary in what they do,* and are a very important component of the game, but are often so different from each other it's hard to summarize them. The best way to learn more about how they work is to read the ones listed on the Monster Cards.

The only consistent rule for special abilities is that if you use a special ability that requires you to roll one or more dice, you are **only required to spend the Energy cost of that Special Ability if one or more dice are successful.** This mechanic exists to make using special abilities less punishing.

Special Abilities that aren't used during a monster's activation don't cost an action token to use, unless specified otherwise.
