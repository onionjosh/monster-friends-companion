**Monster Friends** isn't a game that needs a lot of terrain to be functional, but terrain helps create a more *immersive* looking environment.

A handful of small terrain pieces scattered on a mostly flat play area is plenty, but if you have an elaborate beautifully crafted board you love to play on, feel free to try playing Monster Friends on that board too.

> **PARTY TIP** — Before the game, you and your opponent should briefly assess the terrain and define what kind of terrain every piece is.

Below are the *currently* **defined types** of terrain in Monster Friends. If you own a piece of terrain you'd like to use but don't see appropriate rules for, feel free to try making your own.

## Small Terrain

**Terrain 1" in height** or less is generally considered to be **Small Terrain**, and is small enough for all monsters to move over without penalty. A monster whose base is obscured by Small Terrain receives the benefit of [[kw:cover|cover]], but it is otherwise generally ignored for mechanical purposes.

## Small Obstacles

[[kw:small-obstacle|Small Obstacles]] are things like barrels, crates, and any small object that could be reasonably moved or carried if a medium sized monster really tried to throw it.

## Impassable Terrain

**Impassable Terrain** is anything that would be too hard for a monster to end its move on or cross over. **Models must path around** impassable terrain in order to get around it. Examples include very tall trees, high walls, and deep bodies of water.

## Stable Terrain

**Stable Terrain** is anything that **you and your opponent agree** a monster **can stand on top of**. This includes things like hills and buildings with ledges that can be climbed onto. There are no specific rules for Stable Terrain other than you can stand on it.

## Standing on Terrain

**Monsters can only end their move** on a surface if they have enough room to fit at least half of their base on it. If it's hard to determine if a monster has enough room to fit their base on that surface, roll a d6. If the result is a 4+, they can (if failed, don't test again on that surface for the **remainder of the game**).
