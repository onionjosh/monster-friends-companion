## CRITICAL HITS

If you roll the maximum number value on the dice you are using (6 for d6, 8 for d8, 10 for d10, etc), that attack is considered to be a [[kw:critical-hit|Critical Hit]].

A Critical Hit deals bonus damage equal to the **Crit Bonus** stat of the attack being rolled. If this isn't enough to beat your opponent's Defense Dice, **the attack still does a minimum of 1 damage, unless they rolled a Crit on their Defense Dice roll as well.**

If an attack is affected by a negative modifier (example: an ability that gives -1 to an Attack Dice roll), **rolling the maximum number value on an Attack Dice still counts as a Critical Hit.**

## Outnumbered!

If two or more **other** friendly monsters are within 1" of an enemy you target with a melee Basic Attack, add **+2** to the Attack Dice roll.

## Ranged Attacks

**Ranged attacks** work basically the same as melee attacks, but instead of being within 1", the attacking monster needs to be within the distance of the **Range** stat of the attack being used.

## Range Distance

**Monsters cannot** use ranged attacks to target monsters within 1" of them, but can target monsters they are not within 1" of while another monster is within 1" of them.

Generally, range attacks will be measured horizontally, but in instances where one monster is elevated on a piece of terrain to be higher than the other, measure diagonally from each monster's base instead.

## LINE OF SIGHT

Line of Sight (abbreviated as LoS) is sometimes annoying to determine in many wargames, and **will sometimes be very annoying in this game as well.** A monster can see another monster if they can draw a line from their model's head to any part of the model they want to target.

**If a piece of terrain interferes** in the way of a Ranged Basic Attack that prevents a monster from seeing over half of a monster they want to target, the other monster is considered to be in [[kw:cover|Cover]], and is harder to hit. Monsters receive **+2 to Defense Dice rolls while in cover.**

**Determining if over half** of a monster is visible is difficult sometimes. If you are extremely unsure of if a monster is in cover or not and cannot reach a decision with your opponent, roll a d6: on a 4+, they are in cover.
