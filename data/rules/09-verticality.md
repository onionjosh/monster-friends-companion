## Climbing

**Monsters can climb up or down terrain** they're in base contact with.

Measure the vertical distance from where the monster is standing to where the monster wants to climb, then the horizontal distance required for them to place their base fully on that surface. If that number is equal to or less than the amount of inches remaining in their move, put the model there.

There might be some terrain features on your board that require a very small amount of movement to be able to climb (usually things like hills). Before the game, you and your opponent can decide if these terrain features require a monster to climb them, or if they should be treated as flat.

## Ladders

If there are any ladders (or potentially ropes) modeled on your board, monsters can use them to climb faster. When a monster moves vertically while in base contact with a ladder or rope, the vertical distance traveled only requires half of the movement stat (for example, a 4" ladder would only take 2" of movement to climb).

## Jumping Vertical

Monsters can jump up terrain they don't have a way to climb. If there is a terrain feature directly above a monster they have no way of climbing, like a ledge with no wall, you can attempt to jump that monster vertically. Roll a d6 and add 1/2 the monster's **Move** characteristic: if the result is greater than the distance, you can move that distance vertically. If the test is failed, the monster takes d3 damage and falls back down.

## Jumping over Gaps

**Monsters can jump over gaps** between buildings as long as they have the movement to do so. Treat moving over the gap the same as moving horizontally as normal, but roll a d6: if the number rolled is greater than how many inches the gap is, the jump is successful (this means rolling to jump over a gap less than 1" is always successful, and jumping a gap more than 6" is impossible unless the monster has a bonus modifier).

If the jump roll is less than the size of the gap, move the monster the number of inches rolled across the gap. **Then — they fall from that position** (see [[rule:falling|Falling]]).
